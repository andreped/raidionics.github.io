ABOUT Raidionics

Software for automatic segmentation and generation of standardized clinical reports of brain tumors from pre-operative magnetic resonance images (MRIs). The most common tumor types are supported: glioblastomas, diffuse lower-grade gliomas, meningiomas, and metastases. In addition, post-operative rest tumor segmentation of glioblastomas and surgical report computation (including extent-of-resection), is accessible.

A description of the different use-cases supported by Raidionics, along with Youtube presentation videos, are available on our wiki.

Segmentation

The different tumor types are automatically segmented using our own trained models based on deep learning. The tumor was defined as gadolinium-enhancing tissue including non-enhancing enclosed necrosis or cysts.

Reporting

The standardized clinical report (RADS) generated automatically includes the following tumor characteristics, all computed in the MNI reference space: volume, multifocality, laterality, location w.r.t. cortical structures (MNI, Harvard-Oxford, and Schaefer atlases), and location w.r.t. subcortical structures (BCB atlas).
The standardized surgical report includes an automatic extent-of-resection value.

Research

The software was introduced in this article published in Frontiers of Neurology. More information regarding the content of Raidionics has been published regarding the deep learning aspect, performances of the segmentation models, validation of the standardized reporting.

Partners

All methods have been developed using MR scans collected from a large group of hospitals: Northwest Clinics (Alkmaar), Amsterdam University Medical Centers, University Medical Center Groningen, Medical Center Haaglanden (The Hague), Humanitas Research Hospital (Milano), Hôpital Lariboisière (Paris), University of California San Francisco Medical Center; Medical Center Slotervaart (Amsterdam), St Elisabeth Hospital (Tilburg); University Medical Center Utrecht, Medical University Vienna, Isala hospital (Zwolle), St. Olavs hospital (Trondheim), Sahlgrenska University Hospital (Gothenburg), Brigham and Women's Hospital (Boston), and Oslo University Hospital.
